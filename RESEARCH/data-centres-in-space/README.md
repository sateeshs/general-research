## Research Question

What is the current state, technical feasibility, economic viability, environmental impact, and regulatory landscape of proposals to build data centres in space (orbital computing infrastructure)?

## Key Findings

1. **At least 8 major entities** have filed plans or launched prototypes for space-based computing infrastructure as of mid-2026
2. **~1.24 million satellites** proposed across FCC filings (SpaceX 1M, Orbital 100K, Starcloud 88K, Blue Origin 51.6K)
3. **Hardware is already in orbit**: Starcloud launched first GPU satellite (NVIDIA H100) in Nov 2025; Axiom deployed ODC nodes Jan 2026; China launched 12 AI satellites May 2025
4. **Technical feasibility is proven at small scale** but faces major challenges: thermal management ("physics wall"), radiation degradation, latency constraints
5. **Environmental trade-offs are severe**: space debris risk (Kessler Syndrome threshold already exceeded), ozone depletion from launches/reentries, light pollution
6. **Market is nascent but fast-growing**: projected $1.77B by 2029, $39.09B by 2035 (67.4% CAGR)
7. **Cost parity with terrestrial DCs requires launch costs dropping to <$100/kg** (currently ~$2,700/kg for Falcon 9)
